Kuelap

Amazonas was the cradle of one of Peru’s major pre-Hispanic cultures: the Chachapoyas. It was here that ancient people built Kuelap, an imposing stone structure protected by an outer wall up to 20 meters high, situated at the top of a mountain amid a rugged landscape. Until recently, the only way to reach this archaeological site was on foot.
